Motivation

Throughout the years of traveling to and from various workplaces and schools, I found we could do better as a society than constant traffic. Through this motivation, I tended toward computer studies for a solution. After continual application of this focus, I entered studies in robotics -- the focus of my later school years in computer science. My studies are presently ongoing in the Interactive Robotics and Linguistics laboratory at the University of Maryland Baltimore County.

Research Interests

My research interest include robotics, natural language processing, and machine learning. Naturally, this lead me to the IRAL Lab as mentioned in my motivation. The IRAL lab is a cross between Natural Language Processing, Robotics, Machine learning, and Human Robot Interaction.

Language Interests

I enjoy connecting with my community, which often expresses itself in the form of language. In undergraduate studies, I took up intermediate mandarin, which I was inspired to take by native speakers as instructors as well as classmates. I found Duolingo as a way to get a sense of immersion into the language. As I moved on to graduate studies, I found friends speaking Hindi, which I found fun to converse with. I also picked Hindi up on Duolingo, and have encouraged classmates to compete with me on the app for experience points. The competition helps me to maintain the daily habit of language learning.
